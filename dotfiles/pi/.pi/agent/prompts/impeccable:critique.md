Conduct a holistic design critique — evaluate whether the interface actually works as a designed experience, not just technically. Use the frontend-design skill for anti-pattern detection.

$ARGUMENTS

## Evaluation Dimensions

1. **AI Slop Detection** — Does this look like every other AI-generated interface? Check against ALL the DON'T guidelines in the frontend-design skill.
2. **Visual Hierarchy** — Does the eye flow to the most important element first? Is there a clear primary action visible in 2 seconds?
3. **Information Architecture** — Is the structure intuitive? Related content grouped logically? Too many choices at once?
4. **Emotional Resonance** — What emotion does this evoke? Is that intentional? Does it match the brand?
5. **Discoverability & Affordance** — Are interactive elements obviously interactive? Would a user know what to do without instructions?
6. **Composition & Balance** — Does the layout feel balanced? Is whitespace intentional? Does asymmetry feel designed or accidental?
7. **Typography** — Does the type hierarchy signal reading order? Is body text comfortable to read? Do font choices reinforce the tone?
8. **Color with Purpose** — Is color communicating or just decorating? Does the palette feel cohesive? Does it work for colorblind users?
9. **States & Edge Cases** — Do empty, loading, error, and success states guide users toward action?
10. **Microcopy & Voice** — Is writing clear and concise? Do labels and buttons remove ambiguity?

## Report Structure

### Anti-Patterns Verdict
Pass/fail: Does this look AI-generated? List specific tells.

### Overall Impression
Brief gut reaction — what works, what doesn't, single biggest opportunity.

### What's Working
2-3 specific things done well and why they work.

### Priority Issues
3-5 most impactful design problems, ordered by importance. For each: what the problem is, why it matters to users, concrete fix, which `/impeccable:*` command to use.

### Minor Observations
Quick notes on smaller issues.

### Questions to Consider
Provocative questions that might unlock better solutions.

## Constraints

- Be direct — vague feedback wastes time
- Be specific — "the submit button" not "some elements"
- Say what's wrong AND why it matters to users
- Give concrete suggestions, not "consider exploring..."
- Prioritize ruthlessly — if everything is important, nothing is
